My Why

At the beginning of this year, I came to the conclusion that, above all else, I was driven to be an engineer by my competitive nature. After completing a full semester and taking several engineering related courses, I am now reassured that my competitive edge is what keeps me going. Countless times throughout the semsester whether it was engineering related or not, I have been given an extra spurt of motivation by comparing myself to others. The last few months have opened me up to new project ideas and tasked me with several new challenges. Every time, I gladly accept the new challenge or project idea because it gives me another chance to compete. A chance not unlike what I experienced growing up, both on the baseball field and in my relationship with my brother.

The only thing that has changed about why I want to be an engineer is my level of desire to do so. This past semester has taught me how much I love to do engineering related thing such as coding a new project or solving a difficult math problem. Ultimately, engineering allows me to compete and that is something that I would enjoy doing for the rest of my life.

As a Successful Student

To me, a successful engineering student is simply one who always competes. I believe that in order to have a fulfilling career as an engineering student, I must be able to look back and know that I did not settle. I do believe that it is understandable not to give 110% effor on every assignment. However, I want to know that I never gave up on a class or assignment because I believed that I was not good enough or that something was above me. I want to look back and see myself as someone who was always pushing for the very top.

Fulfilling this definition of a successful engineering student will allow me to look back on my college years and not regret anything. Furthermore, honing this mindeset during my college years when failure is low risk and easy to come back from, will give me good momentum going into the workforce to continue to compete and push for the top.

As a Successful Engineer

I believe that a successful engineer is someone who is competing to create things that allow for other people to either enjoy life more or be more grateful of their life. The competitiveness of the successful engineer will ensure that they are doing everything that they can to push the boundaries of what's possible, instead of just getting the work done that they need to in order to not get fired. Ultimately, I think that the engineer's responsiblity should be to enhance the lives of the people. So instead of the focus being on creating the most perfect product, it should be on creating somemthing that will give someone the most perfect life.
